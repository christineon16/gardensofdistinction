  • St. Luke in the Fields

    The garden's location and design have made for a warm microclimate. This space, with its abundance of berries and flowers, provides a small but important way-station for migrating birds and butterflies during the spring and fall seasons. Over 100 species of birds and 24 types of moths and butterflies have been recorded

  • Jefferson Market Garden

    Jefferson Market Garden is inspired by a classical design in the spirit of Frederick Law Olmstead.

    "Susan has extraordinary vision. I can't imagine the garden being what it is today without her." George Paulos, Chairman, Jefferson Market Garden

  • Incorporating other Wildlife

    Jefferson Market Garden also showcases other natural elements like fish and butterflies as consistent features of the garden's life. With the help of the Audobon Society, we also recently incorporated a bird garden into Jefferson Market--with bird feeders, a bird bath and bird houses.

  • Garden Paths and Seating

    Pathways introduce movement into garden design. In counterpoint, seating offers an opportunity to linger.
